Does my loft have enough headroom for a conversion?
The minimum usable headroom for a loft conversion is generally considered to be around 2.2 metres from floor to ridge. A site visit allows us to assess the existing roof structure, headroom, and pitch to confirm feasibility before any commitment is made.
What types of loft conversion are available?
The main types are a rooflight conversion, a dormer conversion, a hip-to-gable conversion, and a mansard conversion. The right approach depends on your roof structure, the amount of space needed, your budget, and any planning constraints. We will advise on the most practical option for your property.
Do loft conversions require planning permission in Scotland?
Rooflight conversions that do not alter the roofline often fall under permitted development. Dormer conversions and any work that changes the shape of the roof will usually require a planning application. Listed buildings and conservation area properties have stricter rules. We will clarify what applies to your home.
Is a Building Warrant needed for a loft conversion?
Yes. All loft conversions in Scotland require a Building Warrant. The work must comply with current building regulations covering structural integrity, fire safety, insulation, ventilation, and means of escape. We work with the relevant professionals to ensure full compliance.
Will the conversion be warm enough in winter?
A properly insulated loft room should be comfortable throughout the year. Insulation is specified to meet current building regulation standards as a minimum, and we can discuss enhanced levels of insulation if you want to maximise thermal performance.
How long does a loft conversion take?
Most loft conversions take between six and ten weeks from start to completion, depending on the type and level of finish required. You can remain in the property throughout as the work is largely contained to the roof space.
What access is needed during the build?
Our team will need access to the loft via an existing hatch or a temporary opening, and there will be some materials stored externally during the build. We keep the disruption to the rest of the property to a minimum throughout.
