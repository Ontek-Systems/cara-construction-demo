02/Hidden Space Found

Convert Your Garage

A single or double garage in a Perthshire property is often the easiest way to add a genuine room without touching the existing footprint or navigating complex planning applications. In most cases it qualifies as Permitted Development.

Lee Marshall has converted garages into home offices, playrooms, gyms, utility rooms, and annexes. For clients who also want to expand outdoors, a garden room is a natural companion project. The structural work varies depending on the garage type, whether integral, attached, or detached, but Lee assesses this at the outset and quotes accurately for the specific structure you have.

Insulation, internal lining, electrics, heating, flooring, and joinery are all handled in-house. When the project completes, the room is finished and ready to use, rather than a shell waiting for the next trade to arrive.

Garage Conversions FAQs

Do I need planning permission for a garage conversion?
In most cases, converting an integral or attached garage into habitable space falls under permitted development in Scotland and does not require a planning application. Detached garages and properties in conservation areas may be different. We will confirm what applies to your specific property.
Do I need a Building Warrant for a garage conversion?
Yes. A Building Warrant is required in Scotland for a garage conversion. The works must meet current building regulations for insulation, ventilation, damp proofing, structural integrity, and fire safety. We ensure all elements of the conversion are compliant.
What is the typical cost of a garage conversion?
The cost depends on the size of the garage, the level of finish, and what services need to be installed. Garage conversions are generally one of the more cost-effective ways to add living space to a property. A detailed, itemised quote is provided following a site visit.
How long does a garage conversion take?
Most garage conversions are completed within two to five weeks, depending on the scope and specification. If the conversion involves significant structural changes or the addition of services such as underfloor heating, this may extend the programme slightly.
Can I use the converted space as a bedroom?
Yes, provided the conversion meets the relevant building regulations for a habitable room, including adequate ceiling height, natural light, ventilation, insulation, and means of escape. These requirements are built into the design and scope from the outset.
Will losing the garage affect my property value?
A well-executed garage conversion that adds quality living space typically adds more value than the garage itself. However, in some locations where parking is at a premium, the absence of a garage may be a consideration for future buyers. This is worth thinking about at the planning stage.
Can I add a bathroom or kitchen to the converted space?
Yes. We can incorporate a shower room, WC, or small kitchen area as part of the conversion. This requires additional planning around drainage and services, which we manage as part of the project.
