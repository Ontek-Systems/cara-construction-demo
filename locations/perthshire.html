Perthshire properties do not follow a single template. Lee has worked across all of it and understands what each building type demands.

Extending a stone-built Perthshire home takes care. The new work needs to read alongside the original fabric, not fight it. Lee sources matching materials where possible, plans the structural side thoroughly, and builds extensions that sit as though they have always been there.

03 / Why Choose Marshall & Co.

Built for This County

Perthshire covers a vast range of building types. The Georgian townhouses around Crieff and Dunkeld's Cathedral Quarter. The Victorian stone villas on Perth's residential streets. The solid inter-war semis found in every market town in the county. What they share is construction that demands understanding rather than assumptions. Lime mortar cannot be swapped for modern cement without causing long-term problems. Rubble walls do not tolerate the same insulation approaches as cavity-built houses.

Lee is based in Perth and has worked across Perthshire for his entire career. When he quotes a job in this county, the price is based on what he has seen on site, not a figure extrapolated from a different building type in a different postcode. He has encountered most of the complications this county produces and knows how to account for them accurately before the work begins.

The work gets done right because he is there every day to make sure of it.
